Idea 01 Interception rate

The share of attempts that never complete.

Since early 2023 the Home Office has published the number of people prevented from leaving alongside the number who arrived. Plotting them as a share of all attempts (p / (p + m)) is a single line the Home Office does not headline — it's arguably the most policy-relevant number they publish.

Reads as: rolling 13-week share of attempted crossings that ended on the French side rather than with a UK arrival. The shaded band behind the line shows the absolute volume of attempts (arrivals + preventions) so the reader can tell a rising rate on a small base from a rising rate on a large one.

Idea 02 Seasonality

Eight crossing seasons, on one page.

Crossings cluster in the summer and early autumn — a pattern the current site has to be inferred from a stack of annual lines. A month-by-year heatmap makes the shape of the season visible at a glance, and shows that the season has lengthened.

Reads as: each square is one month; darker = more arrivals. Rows are years; columns are months. Hover a cell for the count.

Idea 03 Weekday

Is there a crossing weekday?

Whether boats favour weekdays, weekends, or certain days of the week falls straight out of BOATS_DAILY but has never been surfaced.

Reads as: average migrants per day, grouped by weekday, across the full 2018–present series.

Idea 04 People per boat

The boats are getting fuller.

The perBoat field is already in BOATS_ANNUAL. Plotting it yields a safety-adjacent story that the site currently leaves unwritten.

Reads as: average number of people per detected boat, each year since 2018.

Idea 05 Cumulative

Every crossing, ever.

A single sweeping line from 1 January 2018 to today, tagged at each fifty-thousand-person milestone. Gives readers a landmark memory — "we passed 200,000 in early 2026" — instead of an abstract count.

Reads as: running total of detected arrivals. Labelled points are the dates the cumulative count first crossed 50k, 100k, 150k and 200k.

Idea 06 Context

This week, ranked against every equivalent week since 2018.

The homepage already shows "last week: n crossings". Adding a ranked comparison against the same week of year in every prior year turns a number into a memorable story — 2nd-busiest, 6th-busiest, unprecedented.

Reads as: each bar is the same calendar week in a different year. Current year highlighted. Reader sees rank and margin in one glance.

Idea 07 Divergence

Grant rate isn't one number — it's twelve.

The site's Flow view aggregates all nationalities into one grant-rate figure. NAT_GRANT_ANNUAL already stores the per-nationality series; small-multiple sparklines reveal where the headline rate is pulling from.

Reads as: one miniature line per nationality, each with its own y-axis (0–100%). Fully-comparable shapes, not directly-comparable heights.

All charts drawn client-side from the site's own globals: BOATS_DAILY, BOATS_WEEKLY, BOATS_MONTHLY, BOATS_ANNUAL, NAT_GRANT_ANNUAL. No pipeline work needed to ship any of these.
